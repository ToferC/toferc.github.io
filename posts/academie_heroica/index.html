Hero School

The World of Heroica

Heroica is a verdant world with high magic potential and naturally occuring portals to other worlds through points of great natural beauty and power (waterfalls, mountains, ocean depths, caverns. Portals can also occur close to extremely powerful animals, monsters, spirits and gods.

The portals to the near worlds are open - which means lots of people know about them and anyone can use them to travel. There are also secret portals that require a special key to use. Keys can be kind of weird: Someone walking through the Arch of Vulcan carrying a Phoenix feather will activate the portal to the Elemental World of Fire.

The technology level of Heroica varies highly. The baseline is somewhat anachronistic at a 1400-1500s era middle-ages period on Earth, but with more advanced science, vehicles and computers that nevertheless haven’t resulted in massive industrialization.

As a crossroads, all cultures mingle in Heroica. You’ll find Far West gunslingers walking alongside robotic martial artists, ninja and mad scientists.

Similarly, there are a huge number of different races, cultures and origins here. Talking sentient animals work and play alongside with aetherial gods, artificial intelligences, alien species, humankind and shapechangers.

In short, between the magic and technology, nothing is impossible.

The Hero School - Lycee Heroica

As a natural interdimensional crossroads, Heroica is the natural place to grow and educate the heroes that each world will need to protect its peace, people and environment. Talented youth for hundreds of worlds come to the Lycee every year to learn from and with the best of the best.

Training heroes starts very young, at about six years of age and the training continues until the young adults start their own career as adventurers and heroes on their own.

There are rumours of a foil to this school, the hidden Lycee Villainica, but surely this is just idle talk.

Game Rules

Going for a mix between Cypher System and Nights Black Agents here.

Grade and Experience

Your grade is your level in Academie Heroica and shows how much Effort you can use to make a challenge easier. It starts at 1. You improve your grade through experience. You’ll normally get between 2 and 4 experience for each session you play depending on how much fun you have, how much cool stuff you do and how much you learn.

You can spend 4xp to gain an Improvement for your character. When you improve, you can choose one thing from the following list. You can only choose each improvement once per grade. When you’ve taken all four improvements for your grade, your grade increases by one and then you start again. You can mark your current experience at the bottom of your character sheet.

The improvements are:

  • Increase one of your Pools by one
  • Increase your Effort by one
  • Learn a new Skill at Trained or increase an already existing skill to Specialized
  • Gain a new piece of Gear, upgrade an existing piece of gear or learn a new Knack or use of your Powers

When you’ve taken each of these options once, your Grade improves by one!

Handing out Experience

Heroes get experience for learning, challenging themselves and for being heroes. Each hero gets 1xp for:

  • Having fun
  • Learning something new or trying something totally new
  • Helping or saving someone
  • Failing and learning from it

Pools and Edge

Pools are the way your character will interact with the world. Starting characters will have between 1 and 4 in each of their pools.

Heroes can use effort to reduce the difficulty by your Grade.

  • Might -> Your strength, toughness and physical prowess
  • Speed -> Your agility, speed and dexterity
  • Smarts -> You intelligence, charisma, education and knowledge
  • Courage -> Help others succeed

At Grade 3 and Grade 6, you can choose an Edge for one of your pools. Having an Edge means you can reduce the cost of using the pool by one. This can ease the difficulty of a challenge or activate one of your powers. If this reduces the cost to zero, it doesn’t cost any points from your pool!

Skills

Choose one thing that your hero is very good at. Reduce the difficulty by the skills level when you’re doing these things. You can have up to two ranks in a skill. With your first rank in a skill you are considered Trained and reduce the difficulty by 1. Your second rank in the skill means you are Specalized and reduce the difficulty by 2.

Knacks and Powers

Heroes start with one Knack based on their career or noun descriptor and one Power based on their abilities or verb descriptor.

These knacks and powers make your character unique. You should associate each one with one of your Pools. When you use a Knack, you can spend 1 point from the associated Pool to automatically succeed in a task.

You can use a Power narratively at any time or spend up to your Effort from your Pool for minor to major or rules based impacts.

Equipment

Choose three things that your hero carries most of the time that will help them be heroic. You can use your equipment to make a challenge easier by one level. If you have Armour of some sort, it will reduce damage you take by 1. If you have a weapon, it will inflict 2 damage.

When you improve a piece of gear you can:

  • Add one Armour or damage to the gear
  • Add a special trait like Fiery, Long Range or Explosive that might change how the gear works
  • Add a new ability to the gear, like adding telescopic sight to a hat or jet-pack to armour

Adventures

Welcome to Academie Heroica!

  • Heroes are have just graduated from their home town schools Grade 3 and are starting as Grade 1 Academie Heroica students!
  • They are welcomed to the school. It looks like a massive playground with different cultures and realities all winding around each other.
  • Heroes will see:
    • Unicorns walking on rainbows
    • Talking cats and animals
    • Kids riding flying skateboards
    • Robots and spaceships
    • People practicing magic
  • The new students meet their homeroom teacher for class 1-A.
    • Each student introduces themselves
    • Where they come from
    • What their special “hero” ability is
    • What they like doing the most
  • The first activity: an obstacle course in the “Playroom”! Challenges:
    • A race while dodging paint-balls launched from turrets on both sides of valley -> Speed against difficulty 4
    • Swinging pendelum traps while walking on narrow beams over a pool of “lava” -> Smarts 4
    • Running through tunnels overrun with skeletons and grunt robots -> two successes of Might 4 or Speed 4
    • Leaping over a massive chasm to get to the finish line -> too far to do alone for anyone who can’t fly. Teams need to work together. Team roll: difficulty 4 -> Courage grants re-rolls
